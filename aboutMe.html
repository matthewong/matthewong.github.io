About Me

I am a grade 12 student at Dr. Norman Bethune C.I. Growing up in the middle of the fastest technological advancement period, it was hard not to be fascinated with the everything that was changing. My parents, both being programmers ( Mom actually graduated from the University of Waterloo Math/CompSci ), were my biggest inspiration growing up. After building my first game in Python, I realized the creative freedom and endless possibilities is what makes computer science so versatile. Think about it, you go anywhere else in the world and programming languages are still the same.

Besides programming, another interest that took hold of me late (Grade 12) was finance. I've always avoided Deca but I knew I had to try it in grade 12. Joining Deca in grade 12 helped me realize my interest in finance. The programming applications for financial products and such are endless. My goal is to pursue computer science and finance in the future.

I also keep myself busy with other hobbies too. When I'm not programming or studying, I enjoy morning runs, practicing bassoon/saxophone and watching lots of film.

Music

I play bassoon in my school's concert band and baritone saxophone jazz ensemble. Both are under the direction of Paul Sylvester. In 2015, I had the opportunity to play bassoon with the Toronto Youth Symphonic Wind Orchestra (TYSWO) under the direction of Dan Horner. I am also a part of the UTSC Alumni & Community Concert Band during the summer under the direction of Pratik Ghandi. This year, I'm playing with the OCMS Youth Symphony Orchestra under the direction of Samantha Chang. As it's my first time playing in a string orchestra, I have to say that the it's a lot more different, and challenging than concert band. I've been encouraged by my music teachers to learn as many instruments as I can while I'm young so that's what I do. In addition to Bassoon and Saxophone, I also play flute and piano.

Finance/Deca

I've garnered a great interest in finance during my last year in high school when I took a financial analysis course. Now it's part of my daily routine to check up on the current state of the market and the affairs surrounding our economy. In addition to keeping myself financially literate and up to date with the current affairs, I also joined my school's Deca chapter to capitalize on the learning opportunities and experiences that I would get from my time with Deca.

My final year of high school has definitely changed the way I think and look at the world. I've become far more financially literate and I have a better understanding of the operations and functions of our economy.
